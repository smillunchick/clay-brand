## Design Context

### Users
Local Israelis looking for quality handmade homewares. They encounter Clay at Friday markets, fairs, and online. They're browsing — not on a mission. The job to be done is finding a vessel that feels right for their table, their home, their daily rituals. They value authenticity over polish.

### Brand Personality
**Warm · Sincere · Down-to-earth**

The neighborhood potter. Speaks from the workshop, not the gallery. The name is a bilingual pun: "Clay" / "כלי" (vessel) / "כלי קודש" (holy vessels) — everyday objects with sacred resonance.

Voice: direct, grounded, no marketing language. "Handmade in Israel" not "artisanally crafted ceramic experiences."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Earthy & tactile** — you can almost feel the clay. Texture-forward, material-honest. The interface should have the warmth of unglazed bisque and the quiet weight of a handmade bowl.

- **Palette:** Terracotta `#A25D39` on warm cream `#F2EFE4`. Glaze blue `#4A6B7A` earned only for interactive elements. See `design-tokens.css` for full system.
- **Typography:** Cormorant (display — high contrast, calligraphic affinity) + DM Sans (body — warm geometric). Perfect Fourth scale (1.333).
- **Theme:** Light only. Cream backgrounds, warm neutrals. No dark mode.
- **Logo:** Hebrew-influenced calligraphic wordmark. Counter fill must match background.
- **References:** Israeli ceramic tradition, Friday market stalls, uncoated paper, kiln-fired surfaces.
- **Anti-references:** Gradients, drop shadows, neon, stock photography, machine-made uniformity, the word "luxury."

### Design Principles

1. **Material honesty** — Every surface should feel like it has a physical counterpart. Cream like parchment. Terracotta like fired clay. Texture earns its place, decoration doesn't.

2. **Earned color** — Monochrome (terracotta + cream) is home. The glaze blue appears only when its absence would be a specific loss — actions, links, interactive states. Never decoration.

3. **Imperfection as intent** — Organic irregularity is a feature. Perfect symmetry, pixel-perfect grids, and machine-smooth surfaces contradict the brand. Subtle texture, breathing whitespace, unhurried rhythm.

4. **Quiet confidence** — The design doesn't compete for attention. Large type that occupies space with authority, not volume. Generous margins. Things that reward a second look.

5. **Workshop over gallery** — Show the process, not the pedestal. Hands, clay, texture close-ups. The interface should feel like visiting the potter's studio, not a museum gift shop.
